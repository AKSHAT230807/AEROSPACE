Overview

Aerospace is a field that encompasses the design, development, and manufacturing of aircraft and spacecraft. It is a multidisciplinary domain that combines advanced engineering, cutting-edge technology, and innovation to explore and utilize the skies and space. From commercial air travel to space exploration, aerospace plays a pivotal role in shaping the future of transportation, communication, and scientific discovery. With a focus on sustainability and efficiency, the aerospace industry continues to push the boundaries of what is possible, inspiring humanity to reach for the stars.

In most industrial countries, the aerospace industry is a cooperation between the public and private sectors. For example, several states have civilian space programs funded by their governments, such as NASA in the United States, ESA in Europe, CSA in Canada, ISRO in India, JAXA in Japan, Roscosmos in Russia, CNSA in China, SUPARCO in Pakistan, ISA in Iran, and KARI in South Korea.

Alongside these public space programs, many companies produce technical tools and components such as spacecraft and satellites. Some renowned companies in the field include Boeing, Airbus, SpaceX, Lockheed Martin, RTX Corporation, MDA, and Northrop Grumman. These companies also contribute to other areas of aerospace, such as aircraft construction.
